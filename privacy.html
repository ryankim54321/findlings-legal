Findlings ("we", "our", or "us") is a nature-discovery iOS app that helps you identify plants, track your discoveries, and optionally share observations with the community. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, and share information when you use the app.

Information we collect

  • Account information: If you sign in with Apple or Google, we receive your name (if you choose to share it) and an email address associated with your account. Sign-in is optional — the app works offline without signing in.
  • Photos: When you use the Scan feature, your photo is sent to Pl@ntNet (see "Third-party services") for plant identification. Photos of saved discoveries are stored in your account on our backend (Supabase).
  • Location: If you grant location permission, we attach an approximate GPS coordinate to each discovery so we can show it on your personal map and compute nearby-discovery distances. You can disable location precision in the app's Privacy settings.
  • Discovery data: Species name, date, and any notes or journal entries you attach to a discovery.
  • Device & diagnostic data: Standard iOS crash logs and non-personal usage counters (e.g. number of identifications per day for Pro-tier metering).

How we use your information

  • To provide the core plant-identification, collection, and community features.
  • To sync your discoveries across your devices.
  • To enable opt-in features like Citizen Science contributions and public sharing on the community feed.
  • To send notifications you've enabled (streak reminders, new comments, etc.).
  • To enforce fair-use limits (e.g. daily identification quotas for free-tier users).

Third-party services

  • Pl@ntNet (plantnet.org) — photos submitted for identification are sent to Pl@ntNet's API. See their privacy policy at plantnet.org/privacy.
  • Supabase (supabase.com) — hosts our backend, including auth, database, and photo storage.
  • Apple Sign in with Apple and Google Sign-In for optional authentication.
  • Wikipedia — species info pages are fetched from Wikipedia's public API; no personal data is sent.
  • Apple Push Notification Service (APNs) — delivers push notifications.

Sharing and visibility

Your discoveries are private by default. They appear only on your devices and your signed-in backend account. You can opt in to:

  • Community feed sharing: post a discovery publicly in the community feed.
  • Citizen Science: share anonymized observation data (habitat, growth stage, location grid, species) with research partners for biodiversity research.

You can disable these at any time in the app's Privacy settings.

Data retention and deletion

You can delete individual discoveries at any time. To delete your entire account and associated data, use Profile → Delete Account in the app, or email us (see Contact below). Account deletion removes all your personal data from our backend within 30 days.

Children's privacy

Findlings is not directed to children under 13. If you believe a child has submitted data, please contact us.

Your rights

You can access, export, or delete your data at any time via the app's Profile menu. For questions about your data, contact us at the email below.
